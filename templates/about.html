A Spotify-Pitchfork integrated recommendation engine

In this project, I created an engine to recommend music critics.
This is based on the music you listen to on Spotify.


Music critics are biased.

And it's not a bad thing. These characteristics can be used to profile different types of critics. Perhaps, whether critics are “unbiased” is not even the right question to ask.


Matching bias between critics and consumers improves music recommendations.

Music recommendations - a domain with competition. Music journalism sites such as Pitchfork are facing competition from streaming services such as Spotify, as fast-paced algorithms are replacing slow-paced journalism with instant music recommendations.


In this project, I profiled Pitchfork critics by their album ratings and these albums' audio features.

I used Spotify API's audio features such as 'danceability', 'energy', and 'instrumentalness', weighted by album rating, to profile music critics and predict their ratings.


This yielded a proof-of-concept recommendation engine.

This engine (on this hosted Flask app) matches a Spotify user to a Pitchfork music critic, based on closest music profiles. Users' audio feature profiles are similarly generated, but instead from their top 50 most-played Spotify tracks from the past few months.


That way, music journalism goes on, and we can still have our music recommendations.
